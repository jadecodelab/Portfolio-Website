## Problem

Medical office voice bots need to handle callers who do not follow a perfect script. A patient might interrupt, ask about availability in a vague way, or provide information out of order. Manual testing can catch the obvious paths, but it is slow and easy to repeat the same happy-path calls.

## Approach

I built PGAI Voice Bot Tester as an automated way to simulate patient calls into a medical office voice bot. The tester can run repeatable call scenarios, record the conversation, and make it easier to compare how the bot responds across normal flows and edge cases.

## Tech Decisions

Python made sense for the automation layer because it is quick to script, easy to extend, and works well for testing workflows. Twilio handles the phone-call side of the project, while the testing logic focuses on scenario design, call flow, and the conversation artifacts needed to debug bot behavior.

## What I'd Improve

I would add a clearer reporting layer that summarizes each test run, highlights failed or suspicious moments, and groups issues by scenario. I would also expand the scenario library so the tester covers more realistic patient behavior, including interruptions, repeated questions, and incomplete information.
