I wanted a journal app to document my journey and make it easy to reflect over time. This app is built for my personal use and is intended for long-term use.

I know using AI with a journal might not sound right to everyone, but it's exactly what I want and what I find helpful. The AI isn't there to polish or rewrite my writing. Instead, it organizes my journal entries and helps me reflect on them over time more accurately and efficiently.

This app was built with Claude. My main role was coming up with the idea, deciding on the features I wanted, and deploying it on Railway. It was my first time deploying with Railway. Unlike Vercel, where you can just connect a GitHub repository and hit deploy, Railway required more setup, including configuring environment variables, connecting to PostgreSQL, and integrating the OpenAI API.

It felt so rewarding to finally see the app live. Even though it's a web app, it looks great on mobile and feels almost like a native app. I also added a custom app icon and a web manifest, so I can install it on my desktop and phone home screen for quick access, making it feel even more like a real application.
After testing it, I found a few bugs. For example, the journal entry dates didn't always match the day I wrote them, and the timeline layout wasn't consistent with the other pages. I also improved the user experience by making the app automatically return to the homepage after deleting a journal entry. Before, it stayed on the deleted page, which made it confusing to tell whether the entry had actually been deleted.

Now I can write my journal anywhere and anytime. I'm really happy and excited about it. Let's see how well it holds up over time. I hope that by the end of the year, it gives me meaningful insights into my journey, my growth, and my reflections.
