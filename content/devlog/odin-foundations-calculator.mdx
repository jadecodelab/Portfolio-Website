That's my Foundations capstone for The Odin Project. **A calculator, built from scratch with vanilla HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.**

A few things I'm genuinely proud of in this one: chained calculations that behave like a real calculator should, full keyboard support, a running expression line so you can actually see what you typed instead of just the current number, and because I can't help myself, design background and all - a little 🍀 lucky button.

I also went back in after it was done and fixed a couple of things that bugged me: long results were overflowing the display box, so I added dynamic font sizing. And the display only ever showed one number at a time, which made chained math hard to follow, so I added that expression line just because I love to improve the user experience.

Here's the honest part though. Getting into tech right now, with AI everywhere, is a strange place to start from. It's hard to know what's actually worth learning deeply versus what you can just prompt your way through. The Odin Project became the place where I could ground myself and actually hone my skills, with AI as a learning assistant rather than something doing the work for me. Finishing Foundations and looking at this calculator, I feel proud and motivated to keep going.

None of this would've happened without the people behind The Odin Project,
volunteers building and maintaining a free, genuinely well-thought-out
curriculum.

Onto Full Stack JavaScript next. More of these as I go.
